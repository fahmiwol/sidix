## Novel Methods Claimed

The following methods, architectures, and patterns are first disclosed publicly in this repository under MIT License (see `LICENSE`):

### Foundational architecture (whitepaper v1, April 2026)
1. **Hafidz Ledger** — distributed knowledge-preservation architecture for AI, modelled on Quranic oral preservation system (1,400-year empirical validation). See `docs/sidix-hafidz-ledger-whitepaper.pdf` and `docs/whitepaper/SIDIX_WHITEPAPER_v2.md`.
2. **Proof-of-Hifdz (PoH)** — knowledge-integrity consensus mechanism. Nodes earn participation by proving faithful storage and reproduction of network knowledge, analogous to Hafidz earning Ijazah.
3. **DIKW-H Pyramid** — extension of classical DIKW model with fifth tier (Hikmah / applied wisdom) for AI epistemology.
4. **Maqashid Evaluation Layer** — five-axis quality framework for AI output derived from Islamic jurisprudence (treated as universal engineering principles, not religious doctrine).
5. **Static-Generative Pattern** — cross-domain architectural invariant linking Quranic preservation, DNA, brain memory, and machine cognition. See research note 280.

### Empirical contributions (whitepaper v2, April 2026)
6. **Weight-Level Persona Stylometry** — first published Indonesian-native LoRA adapter encoding multiple distinct voices (5 persona: UTZ/ABOO/OOMAR/ALEY/AYMAN) at parameter level rather than prompt level. Sprint 13 deliverable (`huggingface.co/Tiranyx/sidix-dora-persona-v1`).
7. **Cloud-Iteration Cost Law** — `iteration_cost ∝ opacity(platform) × depth(assumption_stack)` — empirically grounded by 14-iteration journal (Sprint 13). 11-class `ErrorCategory` taxonomy with 7/7 classifier precision. See `apps/brain_qa/brain_qa/cloud_run_iterator.py` and research note 288.

### Compound methods (April 2026, post-whitepaper)
8. **Conversation-as-Training-Data Loop (CTDL)** — external AI sessions (Claude/GPT/Gemini) treated as *guru* whose output is synthesized into corpus and ultimately into LoRA retrain pairs. See `apps/brain_qa/brain_qa/conversation_synthesizer.py` (Sprint 41) and research note 291.
9. **Pixel-as-Distributed-Sensor (PaDS)** — privacy-conscious browser extension + meta-tag pixel that captures keyword-triggered context (`@sidix`) from public web touchpoints, feeding the sanad chain. See `extension/sidix-pixel/` (Sprint 42) and research note 291.
10. **Approval-Gated Self-Replication (AGSR)** — autonomous developer pipeline where SIDIX builds SIDIX, with mandatory owner-in-loop verdict per change (no auto-merge), persona research fanout for context, and Hafidz Ledger sanad chain per commit. See `apps/brain_qa/brain_qa/autonomous_developer.py` (Sprint 40) and research note 291.
11. **Persona-Mediated Sanad Chain (PMSC)** — runtime AI consensus pattern where each persona LoRA acts as a distinct *rawi* (transmitter), with explicit tension preservation rather than averaging. First operational implementation of multi-rawi hadith consensus methodology in machine cognition. See `apps/brain_qa/brain_qa/persona_research_fanout.py` and research note 291.
12. **Compound Sprint Velocity Pattern (CSVP)** — empirically observed 5-sprint-in-6-hours throughput for solo founder + AI agent pair, attributable to logging discipline + scaffold reuse + parallel execution + standardized plan template. See research note 291.
